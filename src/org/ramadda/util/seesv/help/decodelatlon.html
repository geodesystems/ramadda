This converts a column that is of the form degrees minutes seconds into it decimal degrees equivalent. For example, with this data: latitude,longitude 40° 1' 12'' N,107° 40' 10'' W 40:1:12,107:40:10 40:1:12 N,107:40:10 W 40:1,107:40 Running the commands: seesv:seesv -decodelatlon latitude -decodelatlon longitude Gives: latitude,longitude 40.0,-107.0 40.019999999999996,107.66944444444445 40.019999999999996,-107.66944444444445 40.016666666666666,107.66666666666667 If you wanted to have less precision then call: seesv:seesv -decodelatlon latitude -decodelatlon longitude -decimals latitude,longitude 5 -p test.csv Which gives: latitude,longitude 40.0,-107.0 40.02,107.66944 40.02,-107.66944 40.01667,107.66667
