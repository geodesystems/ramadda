For example, if you want to process 2 files - input1.csv and input2.csv - and write them out as new_input1.csv and new_input2.csv do the following. Note: the "\$" is to escape the "$" on the command line. seesv: seesv -multifiles "new_\${file_shortname}.csv" -p input1.csv input2.csv Results in: new_input1.csv and new_input2.csv Use full name: seesv: seesv -multifiles "new_\${file_name}.csv" -p input1.csv input2.csv Results in: new_input1.csv.csv and new_input2.csv.csv Use file count: seesv: seesv -multifiles "filenumber_\${count}.csv" -p somefile.csv someotherfile.csv Results in filenumber_1.csv and filenumber_2.csv
